5 days ago, Anthropic leaked the source code for Claude Code. Accidentally. 512,000 lines. The complete internal architecture of a product worth $2.5 billion in annual revenue was simply sitting in public access.

Security researcher Chaofan Shou noticed it in the morning and posted a link on X. Then GitHub did what it does best — thousands of forks appeared within hours. Anthropic launched 8,000 DMCA takedown requests. While they were busy doing that, Korean-Canadian developer Sigrid Jin — the very person who spent 25 billion Claude Code tokens in a year — managed to rewrite the entire code in Python before sunrise. You can't go after her with a DMCA.

This is the second similar incident this week. Five days prior, 3,000 internal files were leaked, including details of the unreleased \"Mythos\" model. Twice in one week. From a company valued at $380 billion, preparing for a $60 billion IPO this fall.

Anthropic has always positioned itself as a \"safe AI company.\" Constitutional AI, responsible development, \"we are not OpenAI.\" And yet, twice in a week, they drop the ball like a first-month startup.

I'm not saying they are a bad company — Claude Code is a truly great product, and I use it myself. But there's something telling about a company that talks the most about AI safety being unable to keep its own source code from leaking into the public domain due to a \"packaging error.\"

Competitors — OpenAI, Google, xAI — now have a detailed roadmap of what took years to build. No reverse engineering required. For free.

Whether this changes the actual competitive balance remains an open question. But it will definitely change the conversation around their IPO.
