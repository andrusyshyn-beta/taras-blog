## What Happened on February 5, 2026 and Why Is It a Turning Point?

Over the past two years, Shumer, like many others, followed AI with a degree of skepticism. It seemed like progress was gradual, and the moment when AI could truly replace humans in complex tasks was still far off. But on February 5, 2026, two massive models were simultaneously released: **GPT-5.3-Codex** from OpenAI and **Claude Opus 4.6** from Anthropic. Shumer describes that day as "the click when you suddenly realize the water is already chest-high, even though yesterday it seemed like it was only at your knees."

So what fundamentally changed? Previously, AI could execute a task only if you perfectly and precisely described the instruction. The new models began independently making decisions that require **judgment and taste**. This fortress, which for centuries was considered exclusively human territory — intuition, understanding context, and the ability to make the right choice without clear criteria — has just fallen. Now AI understands what you *actually* meant to say, even with extremely imprecise wording.

## What Numbers Prove the Dangerous Speed of Change?

The organization METR measures AI's ability to work autonomously — that is, how long it can independently perform real work tasks without any human help (compared to a domain expert).

How this autonomy has grown exponentially:
- **A year ago:** AI stopped after just 10 minutes and needed intervention.
- **Six months ago:** It lasted about 1 hour.
- **November 2025 (Claude 4.5):** AI could perform tasks that take a human nearly **5 hours** of continuous work.

This metric previously doubled every seven months. The latest data proves that the pace has accelerated to a staggering doubling every 4 months. In a year, AI will be able to work for entire days. In two years — complete month-long projects from scratch to finish without an operator. Dario Amodei (CEO of Anthropic) publicly stated that by 2026-2027, AI models will be unquestionably smarter than most humans in almost all tasks. If AI reaches the level of doctors, does anyone really think it can't handle ordinary office work? Leading Silicon Valley engineers have already delegated the lion's share of programming to machines today.

## Why Is Recursive Self-Improvement the Scariest Part?

In the technical documentation for GPT-5.3-Codex, there's a paragraph that most people simply scrolled past. OpenAI wrote the most important sentence of the year: *"GPT-5.3-Codex is the first model that played a key role in the process of its own creation. The team used an early version to debug training, deployment, and diagnose system testing results."*

Read that again slowly. AI has started participating in creating the next generation of AI. This triggers a cycle of **recursive self-improvement**: a new version creates a better version, which creates an even more brilliant one, and so on. This is precisely what theories call an "intelligence explosion," where progress becomes so rapid that humanity physically cannot keep up with it.

This troubles founders. Brian Norgard (serial entrepreneur) stated that almost every smart person he knows is extraordinarily worried. This isn't Luddite panic — it's anxiety from people who understand the technology better than others and see it from the inside. Meanwhile, people on the street still measure AI by standards from two years ago, perceiving it as a "chatbot for recipes." The gap between the terrifying reality of AI and what society thinks about it is now becoming more dangerous than the technology itself.

## What to Do If You Realize You've Missed the Moment?

The worst thing is to continue doing nothing and hope that it will somehow pass you by. The difference between people who will thrive and those who will suffer comes down to just one thing: **when exactly they started adapting**. We're still in the "early" phase, but this window is irreversibly closing with each month.

### Concrete Actions Right Now:
1. **Force yourself to work with AI:** Start using AI tools tomorrow. Even if at first you complete a task slower than manually. Your goal right now isn't to save 10 minutes — it's to learn to "swim while the water is still shallow." So that when it reaches your neck, you won't drown.
2. **Shift your focus to your "judgment":** Identify which parts of your work require exceptional human intuitive judgment or access to unique, exclusive information that AI doesn't have. Tasks of the format "follow this rule" will be automated first.
3. **Change your mindset ("From tool to autonomous colleague"):** Stop thinking of AI as a calculator or a "minor assistant." Start treating it as an autonomous colleague capable of taking on massive blocks of complex corporate tasks. Transitioning to this mindset will unlock business opportunities you previously ignored simply due to your own stereotypes.
