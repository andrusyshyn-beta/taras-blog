         ## Header
         The development of technologies is gaining momentum, and one of the most interesting directions is the creation of artificial intelligence that can imitate human behavior. The most famous example of such development is Mark Zuckerberg's creation of artificial intelligence that can create a virtual copy of himself for use in meetings.

         ## TL;DR
         - Mark Zuckerberg is developing artificial intelligence to create a virtual copy of himself.
         - The virtual copy can be used in meetings instead of a real person.
         - This development raises questions about the future of work and the role of humans in a world where artificial intelligence can perform many tasks.

         ## Analysis of the situation
         The creation of a virtual copy of a person using artificial intelligence opens up vast opportunities for business and communication. This can significantly increase the efficiency of meetings, allowing people from different parts of the world to communicate without geographical constraints. However, such development also raises questions about security and confidentiality of personal data, as the virtual copy will possess all the information about the real person.

         In addition, the use of artificial intelligence to create virtual copies of people can change the very concept of work. If a virtual copy can perform tasks instead of a person, then do we need a person to be present at work? This question is particularly relevant in the service sector, where communication with customers is a key aspect of work.

         ## Impact on Autonomous Business
         Autonomous business, which already uses technologies to optimize processes, can significantly benefit from the development of artificial intelligence. Virtual copies of employees can be used for communication with customers, task execution, and even decision-making. This will significantly increase the efficiency of the business and allow humans to focus on strategic development.

         However, the development of autonomous business also depends on the ability to create effective security systems that will counteract potential threats from artificial intelligence. This issue is particularly relevant in the context of growing cyber threats.

         ## Cases
         One example of successful use of artificial intelligence in business is the development of chatbots for communication with customers. Chatbots can answer many customer questions, freeing up human time for more complex tasks. Similarly, virtual copies of people can be used for meetings, presentations, and even training.

         ## Forecast 2027
         In 2027, we can expect further development of artificial intelligence technologies and their application in business. Virtual copies of people will be increasingly used for communication with customers, task execution, and decision-making. This will significantly change the concept of work and communication, opening up new opportunities for business and societal development.
