## TL;DR: Key Takeaways in 30 Seconds

- **Paradigm shift:** In 2026, "Open Rates" are no longer a reliable metric due to Apple Privacy Protection 2.0. The focus has shifted entirely to clicks and conversions.
- **Gmail Primary:** To land in the inbox (not Promotions), you need direct engagement with the subscriber and "Plain Text" format instead of heavy HTML templates.
- **AI filters:** Modern spam systems have learned to detect template-like AI text. Use artificial intelligence for structure and base segmentation, but write copy with your own voice.

## How Has Email Marketing Changed in 2026?

The ROI of email marketing in 2025 was $42 for every $1 invested (according to Litmus). This is still the highest across all digital channels.

But at the same time:
- Average open rates dropped from 25% to 18% over 2 years
- Gmail and Apple Mail strengthened their filtering algorithms
- The "Promotional" tab became a mass grave for most marketing emails

The problem isn't the channel. The problem is how most people use it.

## What to Do About Gmail Promotions and Apple Privacy Protection?

### 1. Gmail Promotions Death

Google updated its email categorization algorithm. Now only emails from senders with real interaction reach "Primary."

**Solution:** Ask subscribers to add you to their contacts and reply to the first email. Any interaction signals Gmail that the sender is important.

### 2. Apple Privacy Protection 2.0

After the first MPP (Mail Privacy Protection), Apple took another step — now it's impossible to track even clicks from Apple Mail on iOS 18+.

**Solution:** The focus shifts from "opens" and "clicks" to conversions (purchases, subscriptions, registrations).

### 3. AI-Generated Content = Spam Scores

Ironically, the mass use of AI for writing emails led to spam filters learning to recognize the "AI voice."

**Solution:** Write with a personal voice. AI is a tool for structure, not for copy.

## Highest ROI Strategies in 2026

### Welcome Sequence (Must Have)

The first 7 days after subscribing have the highest engagement. Use this:

**Day 1:** Welcome + what to expect (personal, from the author)
**Day 3:** Best content (one top article)
**Day 7:** Question to the subscriber ("What interests you most?")

**Day 3 email template (copy and paste):**
> "Hi, [Name]. No long intro today. You're here because you want to improve your results in [Niche].
> Here's my absolute top piece of content that generated the best discussions last year: [Link].
> Be honest — what's the biggest challenge you're currently facing in this area? Just hit 'reply' and let me know."

Replies to that last email are pure gold. It's quality segmentation without any additional tools.

### Behavioral Segmentation

Instead of "everything to everyone" — behavioral segmentation:

| Segment | Criteria | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Active readers | Opened last 5+ emails | Exclusive content |
| Dormant | Haven't opened in 60+ days | Win-back campaign |
| Clickers | Click but don't convert | Offer + case study |

### Plain Text > HTML

Personal emails (plain text) show 2-3x higher open rates than HTML templates.

The reason: they look like a real letter from a person, not from a marketing department.

## FAQ

**What's the best email marketing platform in 2026?**
ConvertKit (now Kit) — ideal for content creators and authors. Klaviyo — the best choice for e-commerce. For starting out and basic automation, the free tier of Mailchimp or Substack is sufficient.

**How to grow the subscriber base without paid ads?**
High-converting Lead Magnet + popup signup form on Pillar articles + mentions on social media. Most of my new subscribers come from "Subscribe to newsletter" in my Twitter/X bio.

**How many emails should I send per week?**
For most niches, the gold standard is once a week. If you're sending more, you either have an incredibly interesting life, or quality suffers and unsubscribes inevitably grow.

**Why are my emails landing in "Spam"?**
Sudden launch of campaigns without "warming up" the IP, no DMARC settings configured, heavy use of templated AI phrases, or too many HTML images.
