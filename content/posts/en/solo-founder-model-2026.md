## 1. Why Niche Experience is More Valuable than Coding Skills?

The best *fit* for the solo founder model is someone who has spent 5-10 years within an industry. You know the \"unwritten rules\" and edge cases that aren't written in any documentation.

**Example:** A recruiter with 7 years of experience knows that 80% of their time goes into initial resume screening. She's not a programmer, but she knows the *logic* of the process. In 3 days, using **Claude**, she can build a tool that automates this screening.

> **Result:** Instead of being an employee, she becomes the owner of a SaaS product she sells to other companies.

## 3. Psychological Resilience: How Not to Break?

The biggest problem for a solo founder is loneliness in decision-making. When a product has 20 users and 3 unsubscribe, it's easy to fall into a panic.

**How AI helps:**
You can use Claude as a \"sparring partner\" for reflection. Upload statistics, describe your concerns, and ask the AI to conduct an objective analysis. Often, it turns out to be just a statistical fluctuation, not a business failure.

## FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

**Can I be a solo founder and stay in an employee job?**  
Yes, and it’s the smartest strategy. Use your salary as venture capital for your micro-business until it starts generating a stable income.

**What is the biggest mistake beginners make?**  
Trying to build the \"next Facebook.\" A solo founder should build the \"best tool for a specific task.\" Scale into the depth of a niche, not the width of a market.

**Where to get the first customers without an advertising budget?**  
Where you're already an \"insider.\" LinkedIn communities, professional Telegram chats, niche forums. Sell a result, not just software. I wrote about this in more detail in the article on [traffic generation](/en/blog/site-in-hours-2026).
