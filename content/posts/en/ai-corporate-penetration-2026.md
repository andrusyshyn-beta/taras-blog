## Where are AI Agents showing the best ROI?

In 2026, three main horizontal use cases dominate, where AI isn't just "helping" but radically changing the business's cost structure.

**1. Code Generation and Debugging (Cursor, Claude Code)**  
Code is the perfect environment for AI: dense data, strict syntax, and verifiable results. Engineer productivity is skyrocketing by 10-20x. This directly confirms my [Solo Founder model](/en/blog/solo-founder-model-2026), where one developer can manage the architecture of an entire product.

**2. Autonomous Customer Support**  
AI agents now operate on clear SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures). They handle thousands of tickets faster, cheaper, and often with higher CSAT than humans. The customer doesn't care who solves the problem, as long as it's solved in 2 minutes.

**3. Vertical Intelligent Search**  
Products like Glean (internal search) or Harvey (legal) have become the standard. A significant portion of AI usage today is essentially replacing traditional search with the delivery of instant, synthesized answers. ## Which industries are the "early adopters" of autonomy?

While the tech sector leads, real surprises are happening in the most conservative niches.

- **Legal:** A lawyer's job involves parsing gigabytes of text. Legal AI startup Eve is already valued at $1B because it handles 80% of the document-related routine.
- **Healthcare:** Automation of medical scribing and administrative tasks. Here, companies aren't replacing massive EHR systems; they are supplementing them with autonomous agents for discrete, niche tasks, as I described in my [Micro-SaaS services](/en/services).

**Direct Answer:**
Industries where the work output is text-based and verifiable (law, auditing, code) are feeling the biggest impact. The latest **GDPval** benchmark showed AI efficiency in accounting grew by 20% in just 4 months.

## Why haven't all businesses caught on yet?

The speed of adoption depends on how "physical" the product is. Industries with heavy stakeholder coordination and high regulatory barriers are currently lagging. However, with the rise of **Long-horizon Agents** and **Computer Use** features, AI is starting to work with complex spreadsheets and presentations, opening the door for the next wave of automation.

## FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

**Will AI replace programmers in large corporations?**  
It's not replacing the specialist; it's changing their role. A programmer becomes an architect who verifies the results, allowing companies not to bloat their headcount while scaling with [AI agents](/en/blog/ai-agents-vs-payroll-2026).

**Which startups are currently most promising for B2B?**  
Those focusing on long-horizon tasks: working with complex business processes that require more than one step. This is the infrastructure for the future wave of autonomous companies.

**How safe is it to give corporate data to AI?**  
Modern platforms (Glean, Harvey) use local or secure model instances where data does not enter the training set. This is a basic standard for Global 2000 in 2026.
