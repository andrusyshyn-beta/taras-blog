## TL;DR: Key Insights in 30 Seconds

- **Manual Control is Obsolete:** Google Ads in 2026 operates on autonomous models like AI Max and Advantage+. Attempting to manage bids manually only drains your budget.
- **Data is Everything:** The algorithm performs perfectly only on First-party data (CRM, Email). Without quality tracking, AI learns from "dirty signals."
- **New Formats:** Search is becoming zero-click due to AI Overviews; the future lies in Demand Gen and ads integrated directly into AI-generated answers.

There's an old joke: A man visits a doctor and says:
"Doctor, it hurts everywhere I touch. I touch my elbow—it hurts. I touch my knee—it hurts. I touch my forehead—it hurts."
The doctor looks at him and says:
"Your finger is broken."

This is exactly how many advertisers feel today. They complain that Google Ads "doesn't work." Campaigns are expensive, and conversions are dropping. But the problem is simple: they are still trying to manually drive a system that was designed to be autonomous.

A few weeks ago, Google quietly, almost without loud announcements, began mass-rolling out AI Max—and if you haven't seen this switch in your dashboard yet, you will soon. It's effectively a "let the AI do everything" button. Bids, audiences, creatives, placements—everything. The market reaction was predictable. Some say—finally. Others say—Google has finally taken away our control.

And here the interesting part begins.

I remember when being a great PPC specialist meant managing thousands of keywords, building massive negative lists, and micro-adjusting bids by the hour. It was a craft. Years of experience and a "feel" for the platform. Today, that specialist has either evolved or is in significant pain looking at their dashboard. Because most of what they spent years learning, the algorithm now handles better and faster.

However, there is one thing the algorithm cannot do better—and never will. That is telling an authentic human story in an advertisement.

A fresh figure that struck me: Google Shopping Ads showed a 40% year-over-year increase in conversion rate. Not reach, not clicks—conversions. This is a radical improvement. And it happened not because advertisers got smarter, but because the algorithm finally received enough high-quality data to learn.

And here’s the paradox: for the algorithm to work well, it needs clean data. First-party data. Your data. Email lists, CRM info, real customer behavior. Most businesses in Eastern Europe don't collect this data systematically at all. They run campaigns, get some results, but without proper tracking, the algorithm trains on junk signals and produces junk results. Then they say—Google Ads doesn't work.

Another trend that few have noticed is Demand Gen. This is a reimagined Discovery. Ads in YouTube Shorts, Gmail, and Discover. Advertiser spend on this format grew 192% year-over-year. Nearly tripled. Why? Because search advertising is getting more expensive, auction competition is insane, and Demand Gen is still a relatively cheap way to catch a user at a moment when they aren't searching yet but are ready to be interested.

Plus, there's another change worth understanding. AI Overview—that blue AI-response block at the top of search—is already actively eating up organic traffic. I wrote about how to adapt to this in my [GEO Manifesto 2026](/en/blog/ai-seo-manifesto-2026). Projections suggest that up to 60% of searches will become zero-click. This is a major challenge for blogs and content sites but an opportunity for advertisers, as ads are starting to appear inside these AI answers.

Let me summarize simply.

Google Ads in 2026 is not an advertising dashboard. It’s a partner you need to know how to talk to. You give it good customer data—it finds more customers. This fits perfectly with the [Solo Founder Model (1 person, 1000 AI agents)](/en/blog/solo-founder-model-2026), where you focus on strategy while the AI handles execution.

Human strategy + Clean data + AI execution—this is the formula that delivers results now. Anything outside of this is either failing or will soon.

## FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

**Is it worth switching to AI Max and Advantage+ or holding onto manual control?**
In 2026, transitioning to autonomous strategies (AI Max) is no longer optional. Algorithms have access to billions of signals that a human simply cannot process manually. Manual control is only useful as a supplementary tool for high-level strategy correction, not for daily bid adjustments.

**Why is First-party data so critical for Google Ads?**
Without data on real customers (CRM, actual purchases), the AI doesn't understand who actually brings profit to the business. It only sees clicks. If you feed the algorithm "dirty" data, it optimizes for random people, leading to wasted budget.

**How does AI Overviews affect advertising costs?**
AI Overviews reduces site visits (zero-click search), forcing advertisers to compete for a spot directly within the AI response. While this can increase lead costs, the quality of such leads is typically higher since they received comprehensive information before ever clicking.
